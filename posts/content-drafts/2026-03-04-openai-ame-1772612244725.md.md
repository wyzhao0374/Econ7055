AI ethics are a mess
OpenAI is amending its Pentagon deal due to perceived sloppiness, according to Sam Altman.
This amendment comes after concerns were raised about the company's partnership with the US Department of Defense.

The deal has been criticized for its lack of transparency and potential biases in AI decision-making.
Sam Altman's admission that the deal looks "sloppy" is a rare display of accountability in the tech industry.

Most companies prioritized AI adoption over responsible AI development, and that's a serious problem.
This lack of attention to ethics will come back to haunt them, and OpenAI's situation is just the beginning.

The amended deal is a step in the right direction, but it's just a small part of a much larger issue.
Can you trust companies to develop AI responsibly, or will profits always come first?
